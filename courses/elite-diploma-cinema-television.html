This program is designed to shape the next generation of filmmakers by immersing them in real-world production environments. This hands-on program emphasizes experiential learning, ensuring that students gain direct exposure to cinematography, directing, editing, sound design, production management, and scriptwriting. By working on active film sets and collaborating with industry professionals, students refine their artistic and technical expertise, building the confidence needed to thrive in the fast-paced world of cinema and television. The program’s structure seamlessly bridges the gap between learning and professional practice, equipping students with the skills necessary to enter the industry immediately upon graduation.

Throughout the year, students engage in intensive workshops and guided production experiences, allowing them to develop their creative vision while mastering industry-standard techniques. The curriculum is designed to provide a well-rounded understanding of filmmaking, integrating technical proficiency with storytelling expertise. Practical training is complemented by theoretical insights, ensuring students not only learn how to execute a project but also understand the deeper artistic and narrative principles that define great cinema.

As part of their training, students work closely with experienced mentors and professionals who provide real-world insights and guidance. The program includes specialized training modules covering everything from production logistics to advanced postproduction techniques, ensuring students receive a comprehensive education. Whether they are directing short films, developing scripts, or managing a set, students graduate with a strong portfolio that showcases their skills and industry readiness.

With English as the medium of instruction and Paris as its backdrop, the program also provides exposure to the city’s vibrant film culture and industry networks, offering unparalleled opportunities for collaboration and career advancement. The Elite Diploma in Cinema & Television is designed for the most dedicated and driven individuals ready to fully immerse themselves in the art and craft of filmmaking. This program is not for the light-hearted or those seeking a relaxed learning experience—it demands focus, discipline, and an unwavering commitment to mastering the cinematic process. Through rigorous mentorship and hands-on training, students develop personal projects that serve as a strong foundation for their careers. By the end of the program, they don’t just graduate—they emerge as industry-ready filmmakers, equipped with the skills, experience, and resilience to make their mark in the world of cinema and television.

Course requirement:

The program follows the European Credit Transfer and Accumulation System (ECTS), with students earning 30 credits per semester, totalling 60 ECTS credits upon successful completion of the one-year fast-track program.

Key Features of Our Bachelor Program:

Industry Standard Hands-on Equipment Training:

Students train with professional cameras, lighting setups, and sound equipment used in high-end productions. This hands-on approach ensures they develop technical proficiency in real-world filming conditions.

Training workshop with ARRI and AATON Field Recorder:

A specialized workshop providing hands-on experience with ARRI cameras and AATON field recorders. Students learn advanced cinematography techniques, camera operations, and professional sound recording.

Training in Industry Approved Production:

Hands-on learning with leading editing, color grading, and sound design software like Avid, Premiere Pro, DaVinci Resolve, and Pro Tools.

Practical Classes:

Practical classes are an integral part of the program where students receive hands-on training in essential areas, including camera operation, lighting, sound-on-set, editing, color grading, and sound mixing, ensuring they develop both technical and creative skills.

Personalized Mentorship:

One-on-one tutoring is provided for each student throughout the development of their final-year project, ensuring tailored guidance and support.

Film Production:

The final three months are dedicated to the production of student short films. This immersive project allows students to apply their acquired knowledge and skills in a professional context, creating a tangible portfolio of work.

Project Completion & Presentation:

Throughout their studies, students will be required to complete and present various projects or finished works. This continuous assessment ensures that students develop a comprehensive portfolio showcasing their growth and achievements.

Personal Showreel/Portfolio:

By the end of the program, each student will have developed a personal showreel, showcasing their best work throughout their studies. This showreel will serve as a vital tool for students as they enter the professional world.

HOW TO APPLY

Requirement: Admission Requirements for Elite Diploma in Cinema & Television

High school/Under/Post Graduate applicants are people who have already completed a school or college or university degree and are applying for further studies or job opportunities or new endeavours.

Applicants who wish to apply for the Elite Program, must meet the following criteria:

  • A copy of your passport.
  • Academic Qualifications: Baccalaureate or Equivalent (e.g., High School Diploma, 12th Grade Degree, Under Graduate Degree, Post Graduate Degree, etc.). A copy of the diploma must be provided.
  • Academic Reports for the 1st and 2nd Semester Alternatively, (if currently studying in High School or an equivalent program) must be submitted.
  • Transcript (e.g., High School, 12th Grade, Under Graduate Degree, Post Graduate Degree, etc.).

Application Documents:

  • A Letter of Motivation explaining the applicant’s interest in the program and career aspirations.
  • Presentation of Personal Work, showcasing any previous creative or academic projects.
  • One-Page Write-up: Applicants are required to submit a one-page write-up detailing their future dream project or an outline of a project they would like to pursue.
  • One-Minute Video: Applicants must submit a one-minute video introducing themselves and should briefly present their background, motivations, and why they are interested in DBIMA.

Registration Fees:

Registration fee payment is required once the applicant is selected.
