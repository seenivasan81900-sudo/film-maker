BTS Métiers de l'Audiovisuel – Option Gestion de Production. Our Bachelor's program aims to train a new generation of cinema and audiovisual professionals by combining theoretical knowledge with essential hands-on skills. The curriculum is designed to ensure students can seamlessly transition into the film and audiovisual industries. Over the course of three years, students engage in a well-balanced mix of academic study and practical training, learning from industry professionals with both teaching expertise and real-world experience.

At DBIMA, students gain experience working with industry-standard equipment under the guidance of faculty who are actively involved in the field. This hands-on approach ensures graduates are proficient in current professional techniques. As an international cinema school, DBIMA also emphasizes the global relevance of its training, preparing students for international productions while focusing on European cinematic aesthetics and the distinct French film tradition.

The program spans three years, with English as the medium of instruction. We seek dedicated and passionate students who are eager to immerse themselves in this intensive learning experience. Our ideal candidates are focused, determined, and deeply committed to mastering the craft. Discipline and a strong work ethic are essential qualities we value in students who aspire to take on this journey in this program.

Course requirement:

The program follows the European Credit Transfer and Accumulation System (ECTS), with students earning 60 credits per academic year. This includes 30 credits per semester, totalling 180 ECTS credits upon successful completion of the three-year program.

Key Features of Our Bachelor Program:

Masterclasses with Industry Professionals:

Regularly organized masterclasses provide students with the invaluable opportunity to meet and learn from seasoned professionals. These sessions offer insights into career paths, decision-making processes, and practical experiences in the industry.

Practical Classes:

Practical classes are an integral part of the program and are held at least twice a week. Students receive hands-on training in essential areas, including camera operation, lighting, sound-on-set, editing, color grading, and sound mixing, ensuring they develop both technical and creative skills.

Short Film Production:

Each year, the final three months are dedicated to the production of student short films. This immersive project allows students to apply their acquired knowledge and skills in a professional context, creating a tangible portfolio of work.

Project Completion & Presentation:

Throughout their studies, students will be required to complete and present various projects or finished works. This continuous assessment ensures that students develop a comprehensive portfolio showcasing their growth and achievements.

Grading Criteria:

Grading is based on multiple factors, including class attendance, involvement, practical application, and theoretical understanding. This holistic approach ensures that students are evaluated on their overall commitment and progress in the program.

Personal Showreel/Portfolio:

By the end of the program, each student will have developed a personal showreel, showcasing their best work throughout their studies. This showreel will serve as a vital tool for students as they enter the professional world.

BA CURRICULUM:

Bachelor of Arts in Cinema and Audiovisual – First Year

The curriculum is designed for total immersion in the filmmaking process, blending hands-on training with essential theoretical foundations. Students engage in real-world production environments, mastering core disciplines such as directing, cinematography, editing, sound design, production management, and screenwriting. The program follows a structured yet dynamic approach, where learning happens on set, in studios, and through collaborative projects. With a balance of mandatory courses, electives, and industry-led workshops, the curriculum ensures students develop both technical expertise and creative storytelling abilities, preparing them for professional careers in cinema and television.

Bachelor of Arts in Cinema and Audiovisual – Second Year

The second year of the Bachelor program builds on the skills learned in the first year, focusing more on practical application and specialization. Students will refine their technical and creative abilities through hands-on experience in various aspects of filmmaking, such as camera operation, lighting, sound, and editing. The curriculum combines advanced courses, professional training, and industry exposure, allowing students to apply theoretical knowledge to real-world production scenarios and preparing them for the next step in their filmmaking careers.

The third year of the Bachelor program is focused on advanced filmmaking techniques and specialization, preparing students for professional work in the film and audiovisual industries. HOW TO APPLY

Requirement: Admission Requirements for Bachelor of Arts in Cinema and Audiovisual

Freshers are first-year applicants who are usually current or recently graduated high school students or post-secondary institutions and have not attended any college or university before. To be eligible for admission into the first year of the Bachelor of Arts in Cinema and Audiovisual program, applicants must meet the following criteria:

  • A copy of your passport.
  • Academic Qualifications: Baccalaureate or Equivalent (e.g., High School Diploma, 12th Grade Degree, A Levels, BTEC National Diploma, etc.). A copy of the diploma must be provided.
  • Alternatively, Academic Reports for the 1st and 2nd Semester (if currently studying in High School or an equivalent program) must be submitted.
  • Transcript (e.g., High School, 12th Grade, A Level, etc.).

Application Documents:

  • A Letter of Motivation explaining the applicant’s interest in the program and career aspirations.
  • Presentation of Personal Work, showcasing any previous creative or academic projects.
  • One-Page Write-up: Applicants are required to submit a one-page write-up detailing their future dream project or an outline of a project they would like to pursue.
  • One-Minute Video: Applicants must submit a one-minute video introducing themselves and should briefly present their background, motivations, and why they are interested in DBIMA.

Registration Fees:

Registration fee payment is required once the applicant is selected.

A transfer student is someone who has earned credits at one school or institution but wants to move to a different one while keeping their previous academic performance and credits earned.
