Our Master’s program is designed to cultivate the next generation of cinema and audiovisual professionals by integrating advanced theoretical knowledge with essential hands-on expertise. The curriculum is designed to ensure students can seamlessly transition into the film and audiovisual industries. Over the course of two years, students develop a unique and personalized artistic vision, culminating in the professional application of their final-year project. This individualized approach ensures that graduates acquire the skills, knowledge, and experience necessary to make meaningful contributions to the industry. Through a rigorous curriculum that balances academic study with intensive practical training, students work closely with industry professionals who bring both teaching expertise and real-world experience.

In the second year of the program, students choose a specialization that aligns with their career aspirations. The available specializations include Cinematography, Directing, Production, Postproduction, and Sound Postproduction. This structure allows students to refine their expertise in a specific domain while continuing to work with industry-standard equipment under the guidance of experienced faculty. For instance, those focusing on scriptwriting will engage in the complete development of a feature film project, from initial concept to full scriptwriting, gaining a comprehensive understanding of the craft and its industry demands.

As an international cinema school, DBIMA emphasizes the global relevance of its training, preparing students for international productions while fostering an appreciation for European cinematic aesthetics and the rich French film tradition.

The program spans two years, with English as the medium of instruction attracting a diverse and passionate student body eager to push the boundaries of cinema and audiovisual storytelling. We seek dedicated individuals with a strong work ethic, discipline, and a commitment to artistic and technical excellence. Through one-on-one mentorship, students have the opportunity to develop a personal project that may evolve into their key professional work, ensuring they leave the program with a strong foundation for a successful career.

Course requirement:

The program follows the European Credit Transfer and Accumulation System (ECTS), with students earning 60 credits per academic year. This includes 30 credits per semester, totalling 120 ECTS credits upon successful completion of the two-year program.

Key Features of Our Bachelor Program:

Masterclasses with Industry Professionals:

Regularly organized masterclasses provide students with the invaluable opportunity to meet and learn from seasoned professionals. These sessions offer insights into career paths, decision-making processes, and practical experiences in the industry.

Practical Classes:

Practical classes are an integral part of the program and are held at least twice a week. Students receive hands-on training in essential areas, including camera operation, lighting, sound-on-set, editing, color grading, and sound mixing, ensuring they develop both technical and creative skills.

Pitching & Professional Training:

Pitching courses, along with dedicated pitching training and mentoring, are offered to prepare students for presenting their projects and ideas in the professional arena.

Film Production:

Each year, the final three months are dedicated to the production of student short films. This immersive project allows students to apply their acquired knowledge and skills in a professional context, creating a tangible portfolio of work.

Project Completion & Presentation:

Project Completion & Presentation: Throughout their studies, students will be required to complete and present various projects or finished works. This continuous assessment ensures that students develop a comprehensive portfolio showcasing their growth and achievements.

Grading Criteria:

Grading is based on multiple factors, including class attendance, involvement, practical application, and theoretical understanding. This holistic approach ensures that students are evaluated on their overall commitment and progress in the program.

Personal Showreel/Portfolio:

By the end of the program, each student will have developed a personal showreel, showcasing their best work throughout their studies. This showreel will serve as a vital tool for students as they enter the professional world.

HOW TO APPLY

Requirement: Admission Requirements for Bachelor of Arts in Cinema and Audiovisual

Applicants who wish to apply for direct entrance into the 2nd or 3rd Year of the Bachelor of Arts in Cinema and Audiovisual, must meet the following criteria:

  • A copy of your passport.
  • Academic Qualifications: A Bachelor’s degree or its equivalent (licentiate, undergraduate diploma, etc.) in a relevant field. A copy of the diploma must be provided.
  • Equivalence in Credits: (180 ECTS) corresponding to three years of studies in Cinema, Audiovisual, Mass Media, or Communication at an accredited arts school.
  • Transcript: Academic transcripts for the entire three years of study must be submitted.

Application Documents:

  • A Letter of Motivation explaining the applicant’s interest in the program and career aspirations.
  • Presentation of Personal Work, showcasing any previous creative or academic projects.
  • One-Page Write-up: Applicants are required to submit a one-page write-up detailing their future dream project or an outline of a project they would like to pursue.
  • One-Minute Video: Applicants must submit a one-minute video introducing themselves and should briefly present their background, motivations, and why they are interested in DBIMA.

Registration Fees:

Registration fee payment is required once the applicant is selected.
