The One-Month Filmmaking Program at DBIMA-Paris offers an exceptional opportunity for international film students to immerse themselves in the process of creating a film in the iconic and inspiring city of Paris. Specifically designed for students enrolled in filmmaking courses at partner institutions, this program facilitates a hands-on, step-by-step experience from concept development to final post-production.

Who Is This Program For?

This program is intended for film students from partnering institutions who have already begun their studies in filmmaking and are eager to create their own short film in Paris. By participating, students not only gain practical filmmaking experience but also engage in the creative and technical challenges of making a film in one of the world's most renowned cinematic cities.

The Cost of Filmmaking in Paris

Producing a film in Paris can be financially daunting due to the high costs of locations, permits, equipment, and crew. However, the Filmmaking in Paris program is designed to offer an affordable way for students to create their films in the city. In collaboration with our partnering institutions, DBIMA offers financial and logistical support to help students manage production costs, making the experience more accessible while maintaining a high standard of production.

Program Structure – Curriculum Overview

The curriculum is designed to immerse students in every stage of filmmaking, ensuring that by the end of the program, participants have produced a fully completed film, ready for screening. The structured timeline allows students to develop their skills progressively, from initial concept to final delivery.

Preparing for Shooting a Film

Students begin with an intensive refresher course on filmmaking in Paris, covering:

  • The logistical challenges and advantages of shooting in the city.
  • Navigating French film industry standards.
  • Utilizing Parisian locations for effective visual storytelling.

Additionally, students participate in production workshops focusing on:

  • Casting processes and European filmmaking techniques.
  • Shot listing, storyboarding, and scene breakdowns.
  • Planning and organizing film shoots efficiently.

Pre-Production

This phase transforms ideas into structured production plans. Under the guidance of industry professionals and DBIMA mentors, students work on:

  • Script development and revisions.
  • Casting and rehearsals with actors.
  • Location scouting and securing permits.
  • Equipment planning and technical requirements.
  • Scheduling and budgeting for the shoot.

By the end of this week, students have a fully prepared production plan, ready for execution.

Shooting the Film

Students transition into the production phase, where they work in teams to shoot their films across various Parisian locations. This hands-on experience covers:

  • Directing actors and managing performances.
  • Cinematography techniques and lighting setups.
  • On-set production management and troubleshooting.
  • Collaboration between departments (camera, sound, art direction).
  • Capturing all planned shots within the set schedule.

Post-Production & Delivery

Once shooting is complete, students move into post-production, where they refine their films through:

  • Editing
    Structuring the footage, adjusting pacing, and ensuring narrative coherence
  • Sound Mixing
    Syncing dialogue, adding sound effects, and enhancing audio quality
  • Color Grading
    Refining visual tone and enhancing aesthetics/span>

The final stage focuses on delivery, where students:

  • Export and format their films for screenings and distribution.
  • Learn about film festival submissions and industry-standard delivery formats.
  • Participate in a private screening and feedback session with professionals.

By the end of the program, each student will have a completed short film, marking their first professional milestone in the film industry. Additionally, they will receive a Diploma in Filmmaking from DBIMA, recognizing their achievement and preparing them for further career opportunities in the industry
